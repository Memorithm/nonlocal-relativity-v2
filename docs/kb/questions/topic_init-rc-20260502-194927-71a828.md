# Stage 01: Topic Init

# SMART Research Goal

- **Topic**: Build-working-autonomous-research-tool
- **Novel Angle**:  
  Existing autonomous research agents (e.g., *The AI Scientist*, Lu et al. 2024) rely on massive proprietary LLMs like GPT-4o, requiring expensive API calls and large‑scale compute, which limits reproducibility, accessibility, and trust. No prior work has demonstrated that a **local, small open‑source LLM (≤7B parameters)** can autonomously conduct end‑to‑end deep‑learning research—from hypothesis generation through experiment coding to paper writing—**with results comparable to those of frontier models**.  
  **Why now?**  
  - Small language models (Llama‑3‑8B, Mistral‑7B, DeepSeek‑7B) have reached a tipping point in reasoning and code generation, yet their agentic capabilities remain underexplored.  
  - Efficient inference engines (llama.cpp, vLLM) now allow a 7B model to run comfortably on a single consumer GPU, making fully local autonomous research feasible for the first time.  
  - The push for open science and democratised AI creates a need for tools that do not depend on black‑box commercial APIs.  
  **How this differs:**  
  We introduce an **iterative refinement loop with execution‑feedback and self‑critique** that compensates for the smaller model’s weaker raw reasoning. The agent does not merely generate a plan once; it observes the outcome of its experiments, debugs errors, and revises hypotheses—mimicking the cognitive cycle of a human researcher. This *system‑level* innovation, rather than sheer model scale, is the key contribution.

- **Scope**:  
  We will build **Claw‑AI‑Lab‑Local**, a lightweight autonomous research tool, and evaluate it exclusively on **small‑scale deep‑learning tasks** that fit within a single‑GPU, sub‑6‑hour budget. The scope is intentionally narrow: we target the **AI Scientist task suite** (Lu et al. 2024) because it provides a standardised evaluation protocol and baseline results. The tool itself is designed to be task‑agnostic, but the experimental validation is confined to 5 representative tasks (e.g., *nanoGPT_lr*, *2D_diffusion*, *grokking*).

- **SMART Goal**:  
  - **Specific:** Design, implement, and open‑source a local autonomous research agent powered by a quantised Llama‑3‑8B model, featuring a structured self‑debugging and hypothesis‑refinement loop.  
  - **Measurable:** Evaluate the agent on 5 tasks from the AI Scientist benchmark. The primary metric is the **Automated Reviewer Score** (novelty and validity, each 1–5).  
  - **Achievable:** With a single NVIDIA RTX 4090 (24 GB VRAM), the agent can run the LLM (4‑bit quantised) and the target experiments within 6 hours per task.  
  - **Relevant:** Proving that a small, open model can match GPT‑4o‑level autonomous research would shift the paradigm from “bigger is better” to “smarter system design,” directly impacting the accessibility of AI‑driven science.  
  - **Time‑bound:** Within 3 months (target: 2025‑08‑01), deliver a working prototype, a pre‑registered report, and evaluation results ready for submission to a top‑tier workshop (e.g., NeurIPS AI for Science).

- **Constraints**:  
  - **Compute:** 1× NVIDIA RTX 4090 (24 GB VRAM), 32 GB system RAM. No cloud APIs or external LLM services permitted.  
  - **Time:** ≤ 6 hours wall‑clock time per research task (including LLM inference and experiment runs).  
  - **Data:** Only publicly available datasets (CIFAR‑10, WikiText‑2, etc.) and the AI Scientist task templates.  
  - **Model:** Quantised 7B‑class model (e.g., Llama‑3‑8B‑Instruct‑Q4_K_M). No fine‑tuning; the system must work with off‑the‑shelf weights.

- **Success Criteria**:  
  1. **Primary:** For at least 3 of the 5 selected tasks, the agent generates a complete research paper that receives a mean Automated Reviewer **novelty ≥ 2.5** and **validity ≥ 2.0**, matching the published GPT‑4o baseline (Lu et al. 2024) within statistical non‑inferiority (p > 0.05).  
  2. **Secondary:** The total electricity cost per paper remains under $5 (GPU power draw ~450 W).  
  3. **Publication readiness:** The tool’s architecture and results are documented in a paper accepted at a peer‑reviewed ML venue (conference workshop or journal).  
  4. **Reproducibility:** Code, prompts, and experiment logs are fully open‑sourced, enabling anyone with a single GPU to replicate the entire research pipeline.

- **Benchmark**:  
  - **Name:** AI Scientist Task Suite (subset)  
  - **Source:** [SakanaAI/AI-Scientist](https://github.com/SakanaAI/AI-Scientist) (Lu et al., 2024)  
  - **Selected tasks:** `nanoGPT_lr`, `2D_diffusion`, `grokking`, `cifar10`, `autoencoder` – all modified to fit a 6‑hour limit.  
  - **Metrics:** Automated Reviewer Score (1–5) for *novelty*, *validity*, *clarity*, evaluated by GPT‑4o using the original AI Scientist rubric.  
  - **Current SOTA:** GPT‑4o‑based AI Scientist – mean novelty ≈ 2.5, validity ≈ 2.0 (no small‑model baseline exists).
