# Stage 08: Hypothesis Gen

### Final Synthesis: Three Hypotheses for a Human‑AI Collaborative Research Program

These hypotheses integrate the strongest ideas from the innovator (hallucination‑driven novelty, metacognitive probing), the pragmatist (taxonomy‑constrained prompting, retrieval‑augmented self‑critique), and the contrarian (autonomy as category error, amplification of the reproducibility crisis). They are designed to be tested within a limited‑compute, human‑in‑the‑loop framework—directly addressing the contrarian’s call for assistive rather than autonomous tools while preserving the most radical, testable claims.

---

### Hypothesis 1: Collaborative Hallucination‑Driven Ideation (CHDI)

**Rationale**  
The innovator’s Hallucination‑Driven Divergent Ideation (HDDI) proposes that controlled hallucination can boost the novelty of generated hypotheses without destroying minimal feasibility. The contrarian warns that fully autonomous generation will produce “plausible bullshit” devoid of genuine insight. We synthesize these by embedding hallucination‑injected generation into a *collaborative* workflow: the AI generates highly speculative hypotheses under a high‑temperature, counterfactual prompt, and a human researcher curates, refines, or discards them. This preserves the novelty‑enhancing mechanism while keeping human intentionality and judgment as the gatekeeper, directly addressing the contrarian’s insistence on assistive tools. The pragmatist’s emphasis on feasibility is met by using human expert ratings as the final feasibility filter.

**Measurable Prediction**  
- **Setup**: For 15 diverse research topics, a 7B LLM generates 20 hypotheses under three conditions:  
  (A) Human alone (no AI),  
  (B) AI grounded (low temperature, factual prompt) + human curation,  
  (C) AI hallucination‑injected (high temperature, speculative prompt) + human curation.  
  In conditions B and C, the human selects and refines the top 5 hypotheses from the AI‑generated set.  
- **Metrics**:  
  - *Novelty score*: mean cosine distance of final hypothesis embeddings from the top‑10 most similar arXiv abstracts.  
  - *Feasibility score*: average rating (1–5) by two domain‑agnostic methodologists.  
- **Prediction**: Condition C yields novelty scores significantly higher than both A and B (p<0.01, Mann‑Whitney U), with feasibility scores ≥ 3.0 (i.e., “moderately feasible”) and not significantly lower than B.

**Failure Condition**  
The hypothesis is rejected if:  
- The novelty increase in C over B is not statistically significant, OR  
- Mean feasibility in C drops below 3.0, OR  
- Human curators report that the hallucinated hypotheses are overwhelmingly nonsensical and unusable (qualitative exit survey).

This keeps the human in the loop, mitigating the risk of an echo chamber while leveraging the probe’s ability to surface hidden validity signals. Feasibility is ensured by using a small 1B model and a simple logistic probe, requiring minimal compute.
